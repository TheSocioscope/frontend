General Questions

What is The Socioscope? +
The Socioscope is a transformative qualitative research project that maps how behaviours, norms, and communities influence the transition toward sustainable food systems. We serve as a comprehensive observatory, making knowledge accessible to researchers, policymakers, and practitioners worldwide while shining a light on local initiatives that drive sustainable change.
When was The Socioscope founded? +
The Socioscope was founded in 2022 and is headquartered in Paris, France. Since then, we have expanded our research reach to over 35 countries globally.

Research & Methodology

What research methods do you use? +
We combine academic rigor with participatory research approaches through a four-step process: (1) Identification through network analysis and community nominations, (2) Documentation via in-depth interviews with initiative leaders, (3) Analysis using AI-powered mixed methods to identify patterns, and (4) Dissemination through publications, social media, and community engagement.
How many initiatives have you documented? +
Over 700! Our database includes hundreds of initiatives from grassroots movements, community-led projects, participatory governance experiments, and collaborative problem-solving platforms across 35+ countries. We continuously expand our documentation as we discover new initiatives driving sustainable food systems.
How do you ensure research quality? +
We maintain rigorous quality standards through trained interviewers, standardized protocols, peer review processes, and ethical research practices. Our research team includes leading scholars in sustainable food systems and social innovation, ensuring academic excellence in all our work.

Participation & Collaboration

How can I submit an initiative to be documented? +
We welcome submissions from anyone working on sustainable food systems initiatives. You can submit your initiative through our projects page or by reaching out directly to our research team. We look for initiatives that demonstrate innovative approaches to sustainable transformation in food systems.
Do you collaborate with other research institutions? +
Absolutely. Collaboration is one of our core values. We partner with research institutions, universities, and organizations worldwide. Our team includes scholars from the Institute of Advanced Study Paris, Complexity Science Hub Vienna, and other leading institutions.
How can I stay updated on your work? +
Follow us on social media, subscribe to our newsletter, and check our website regularly for new publications, findings, and opportunities to engage. We share updates on new initiatives documented, research insights, and ways to participate in our growing global community.

Postobón is a 120-year-old Colombian beverage company with 19 production plants and more than 60 distribution centers, allowing it to cover 95% of Colombian territory. The company has developed a sustainability model called “Uno Más Todos,” which structures its efforts around three main pillars: consumer wellbeing, environmental stewardship, and social development.

In the area of consumer wellbeing, Postobón focuses on reducing sugar in its products and promoting healthier habits through programs that encourage active lifestyles. These initiatives aim to support more balanced consumption and raise awareness around health and physical activity.

Environmental stewardship is another key component of the “Uno Más Todos” model. The company implements circular economy practices, climate action strategies, and water management initiatives across its operations. These efforts are designed to reduce environmental impact and strengthen sustainability across the supply chain. Postobón has also achieved carbon neutrality certification.

The social development pillar focuses on strengthening livelihoods and supporting communities connected to the company’s activities. Postobón works with 6,500 recyclers and 1,600 fruit farming families, integrating them into its supply chain and sustainability initiatives. Through these programs, the company aims to promote inclusive economic opportunities while reinforcing responsible production and resource management.

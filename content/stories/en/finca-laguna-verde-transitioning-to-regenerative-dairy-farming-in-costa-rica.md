Finca Laguna Verde is a dairy farm in Costa Rica that has been in operation for approximately 50 years. Around seven to eight years ago, the current owner took over the farm and decided to transition its practices toward regenerative rotational grazing. This shift was driven primarily by environmental concerns, particularly the desire to reduce the farm’s contribution to climate change and restore the health of the land.

The transition to regenerative practices faced strong opposition from the local community and even from the owner’s own family. Despite these challenges, the farm adopted a new management approach focused on soil health and ecological restoration.

The farm maintains a small herd of around 60 cows, with about 30 being milked at any given time. Since the transition, milk production has dropped significantly, reaching about a quarter of previous levels. However, the owner has maintained financial stability by reducing input costs related to feed and medication, resulting in a slightly more profitable operation.

In addition to dairy production, the farm generates income through housing and is considering future diversification. Plans include expanding production and developing value-added products to strengthen the farm’s economic sustainability.
